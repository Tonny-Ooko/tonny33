It was a bright afternoon, and Tony sat in his home office, headphones on, eyes glued to the lines of code on his screen. He was engrossed in reviewing a website design, adjusting colors, tweaking fonts, and testing functionality. As a software engineer, Tony thrived in this world of logic and creativity. The concentration was intense, and the faint hum of his laptop accompanied the clicking of his keyboard.

Then, his phone buzzed. He glanced at it, frowning at the unknown number on the screen.

He debated whether to pick up. Unknown numbers often brought scam calls, awkward wrong numbers, or terse requests for surveys. Yet, curiosity got the better of him.

“Hello?” Tony said cautiously.

“Hi,” came the voice on the other end. It was a warm, friendly voice, though slightly uncertain. “Is this Tony?”

“Yes, this is Tony,” he replied. “Who’s calling?”

“My name’s Brightone,” the caller said. “I hope I’m not bothering you. I was trying to reach Tony—the one I spoke to earlier about a job opportunity.”

Tony raised an eyebrow. “I think you might have the wrong number. I’m not sure who you’re looking for, but I don’t recall any discussions about jobs.”

A pause followed, and Tony could almost feel the disappointment radiating through the phone.

“Oh,” Brightone said finally. “I must’ve dialed the wrong number. I was looking for the Tony who promised to help me find a job. I studied Biochemistry at the University of Nairobi and have been struggling to find something in the field.”

Tony’s interest was piqued. He leaned back in his chair, temporarily setting his work aside.

“Biochemistry,” he repeated, nodding to himself. “That’s a challenging but fascinating field. So you’ve been looking for work since you graduated?”

“Yes,” Brightone said, his tone softening as he spoke. “I’m really passionate about Biochemistry. It’s what I’ve always wanted to do, but opportunities are scarce, and I’ve been applying everywhere with no success.”

Tony listened, feeling a pang of empathy.

Yet dreams are caught in life’s cruel pull.
A passion burning, bright, sincere,
Yet doors stay shut, hope turns to fear.

Life’s path, they say, is rarely straight,

Tony broke the silence that followed the unspoken weight of Brightone’s words. “That must be really frustrating. Have you thought about exploring other fields while you wait for the right opportunity in Biochemistry?”

Brightone hesitated. “Well, yes, actually. I’ve always had some interest in ICT. I even started learning Python and R at one point. But…” His voice trailed off.

“But?” Tony prompted.

“I didn’t finish,” Brightone admitted with a self-deprecating laugh. “I started but never saw it through. Life got in the way, or maybe I just didn’t push myself enough.”

Tony smiled, though Brightone couldn’t see it. “That’s more common than you’d think. But you know, it’s never too late to pick it up again. Python is a great language, and if you’re looking for something manageable, you could try learning Flask. “Maybe,” Tony replied with a grin. “But seriously, you should give it another shot. Even if you go back to Biochemistry, having ICT skills could make you stand out. Employers love people who can bridge different fields. And Python? That’s useful almost anywhere.”

Brightone’s voice brightened. “You know, I think I will. Maybe it’s time to stop waiting for the right opportunity and start creating it.”

As the call ended, Tony returned to his work with a smile, the conversation lingering in his mind.

Sometimes, the world surprises us in the most unexpected ways. A wrong number, a stranger’s voice—these moments have the power to shape our days, to spark new ideas, to turn strangers into friends.

And as Brightone looked at his notebook that evening, ready to scribble down Python tutorials, he, too, smiled. Maybe, just maybe, the wrong call had been the right one all along.

Introduction
Marriage and dating—timeless concepts that remain deeply intertwined with personal, societal, and cultural values—are as complicated as they are fascinating. In today’s fast-evolving world, these ideas continue to be challenged, redefined, and sometimes even rejected. This article seeks to explore the intricate layers of marriage and dating through the lens of unique personal experiences, cultural expectations, and modern approaches.

Dating and marriage have always been complex aspects of human relationships, influenced by culture, personal preferences, and societal norms. A fascinating example of this complexity is the story of a man who took a rather unconventional approach to express his interest in a woman. Instead of directly approaching Shiku, the lady he admired, he decided to talk to her mother first. This bold strategy, perhaps influenced by tradition or a desire to show respect, led to a sequence of events that tested his patience, resilience, and understanding of modern dating dynamics.

After making his intentions known to Shiku’s mother, he had to wait for two days to receive an answer. When he returned, he encountered Shiku, who bluntly rejected him. Insisting on hearing the verdict from her mother, he faced further complications. Her mother, seemingly overwhelmed by the numerous customers she dealt with daily, initially failed to recognize him. Matters escalated when a man in the house, assumed to be Shiku’s father, became confrontational upon learning of his intentions. The situation spiraled into a heated confrontation, with Shiku herself stepping in aggressively. Although the man escaped unharmed, the experience left him deeply traumatized, questioning the effectiveness of his approach.

Reflecting on this incident, one might consider the various factors at play. Was this a case of natural rejection, tribal bias, or miscommunication? His experience sheds light on the challenges of modern dating, where traditional and contemporary values often clash. While his approach was unconventional, it highlighted the evolving nature of relationships, where direct communication and mutual understanding are becoming increasingly crucial.

In today’s world, dating comes with its own set of expectations and complexities. Some advocate for the return of arranged marriages, arguing that they offered stability and alignment of values. However, this approach is not without its critics, particularly those who value personal choice and emotional connection. Another option is referrals, where trusted friends or family members introduce potential partners, offering a middle ground between arranged and self-initiated relationships. Ultimately, many prefer the freedom to choose their partners, although this freedom sometimes leads to repeated cycles of trial and error.

The role of men and women in relationships is also a topic of debate. While some argue for traditional roles, others emphasize equality and shared responsibility. The Bible, for instance, underscores the significance of men leading their families, though this interpretation varies among individuals. Modern relationships demand flexibility and mutual respect, with both parties contributing to their success.

For some, dating is likened to a supermarket experience—selecting and discarding partners at will. This perception, while criticized, reflects the transient nature of some modern relationships. Despite these challenges, the essence of dating and marriage remains rooted in effort, commitment, and shared values. As the Word affirms marriage as God-ordained, it emphasizes the importance of nurturing relationships with care and intention.

Ultimately, dating in the modern era requires adaptability and self-awareness. Whether through traditional methods, referrals, or contemporary approaches, individuals must navigate the complexities with patience and integrity. While the journey may not always be smooth, the lessons learned along the way contribute to personal growth and a deeper understanding of what it takes to build a meaningful relationship.
