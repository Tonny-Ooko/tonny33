Life is either a daring adventure, or nothing. – Helen Keller

What is life without the new?
From dishes to clothes, to cars that woo,
We crave the fresh, the shiny, the bold,
Chasing treasures, stories untold.

Some swap partners like swapping shoes,
Seeking thrills, a fresh muse.
But what does “new” bring to the soul?
Does it truly mend? Does it make us whole?

Curiosity—oh, how it calls!
A siren song in life’s great halls.
From entrepreneurs with their clever spins,
To tech wizards crafting groundbreaking wins.

New ideas, new ways, a relentless chase,
Fueling the progress of the human race.
Yet, pause a moment, and let us see,
Is the “new” as golden as it seems to be?

The mind, it’s wired for exploration,
Thriving on innovation and creation.
Yet every choice, a burden to bear,
33,000 decisions, filling the air.

What to eat, what to wear, what to say,
Countless moments shape our day.
New ideas, they double the stakes,
Making each choice a gamble we take.

A world of dynamics, spinning fast,
From yesterday’s wonders to futures vast.
The subconscious wrestles, the conscious debates,
Which path to follow, what action awaits.

Yes, the “new” controls and propels,
But it burdens and excites as well.
So let us embrace it, wisely and true,
For life’s daring adventure lies in the “new.”


The new—it thrills, it guides, it steers,
It sparks our joys, it fuels our fears.
But with each choice, a chance to grow,
A daring adventure, as Helen would know.
