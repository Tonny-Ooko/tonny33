Wed, 15 Jan 2025 14:00:02 +0000 en hourly 1 http://wordpress.com/ https://s0.wp.com/i/buttonw-com.png software – Tosh https://tonny33.wordpress.com The Frustration of Windows, The Call of Linux https://tonny33.wordpress.com/2025/01/15/the-frustration-of-windows-the-call-of-linux/ https://tonny33.wordpress.com/2025/01/15/the-frustration-of-windows-the-call-of-linux/#comments Wed, 15 Jan 2025 14:00:02 +0000 http://tonny33.wordpress.com/?p=176

In the land of endless clicks and crashes,
Where systems freeze in fleeting flashes,
I sat with hope, with faith in hand,
To conquer Windows, make a stand.

A screen of blue, a sudden black,
Each update came, and then came back.
The spinning wheel, a torturous sight,
I asked, “Why must this be my plight?”

I tried again, a virtual quest,
To install Windows in a peaceful nest.
A virtual machine, they said, could free,
But it failed me—oh, it failed me!

The bugs, the bugs! They never ceased,
Windows promised, yet found no peace.
A world of shenanigans, of endless woe,
Where every attempt seemed to steal the glow.

But then I thought of a distant shore,
A land where freedom reigns, once more.
Linux whispered in the winds so wide,
A place where hope and power collide.

A world of open doors, no chains,
No bloated apps or hidden pains.
“Come back,” it said, “let’s start anew,
No more frustrations, no more blue.”

So, I packed my bags, my Windows dreams,
And thought of Linux as the silver streams.
A place to roam, a place to build,
To craft a world, to shape and wield.

But Linux, too, had its own test,
The installation, I thought, was best.
But there were issues, bugs again,
I tried to fix, I tried again.

The VM failed, just as before,
The struggles mounted, more and more.
I questioned myself, my heart so torn,
Had I made the right choice to be reborn?

Yet through the trials, I learned the cost,
That sometimes battles must be lost.
For each mistake was just a step,
A path I walked, a life adept.

No system perfect, no path complete,
The journey’s long, and quite the feat.
But in the end, the heart will know,
Where to call home, where to let go.

So now I stand, my mind made clear,
Windows has gone, no more the fear.
Linux, the land where freedom flies,
No more the chains, no more the lies.

And to all who struggle, who feel the burn,
Know that with each step, you’ll learn.
The systems break, the updates stall,
But in the end, you stand tall.

For not just machines, but hearts do grow,
And in the darkness, you will know,
That every failure is just a key,
Unlocking paths, setting you free.

Let others learn from this long tale,
The journey’s rough, but do not fail.
For Windows and Linux, both will call,
But you, my friend, must heed the thrall.

Choose what speaks to your inner voice,
And remember: your journey is your choice.
