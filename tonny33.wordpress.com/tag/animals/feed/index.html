Only silence rules, no cries, no chains.
A home of darkness, no flicker of light,
Only the soil knows its story, its plight.

For soil the house is, to soil it returns,
Where time stands still, and eternity burns.
A haven for insects, for rodents to reign,
Blowflies and flesh flies dance in the pain.

Beetles and ants, they gather in cheer,
For this is their joy, their home sincere.
The bones grow dry when love has flown,
All that remains is the echo of stone.

O house with no roof, a silent domain,
You linger in shadows, through sun and rain.
Here lies the truth, where stillness grows,
Where the earth reclaims all it knows.

While it lasts, let it stand, let it be,
A house with no roof, where the soil runs free.
