Ooh, what a mystery, what a sight,
A whisper of wings in golden light.
From fragile eggs on tender leaves,
Life begins—soft as a dream it weaves.

Yes, a change, a wondrous thing,
A crawling form, yet set to wing.
The caterpillar, slow and small,
Yet holds within the promise of all.

Wrapped in stillness, tucked away,
A silent prayer, night meets day.
Cocooned in patience, time must flow,
Till life awakens, set to glow.

And then—ooh, behold the flight!
Colors bursting, pure delight.
The butterfly, a whispered grace,
A love of sky, a soft embrace.

What lessons in this dance so bright?
To change, to grow, to seek the light.
A symbol true, for man to see,
That life’s own hands bring destiny.

With wings of joy, the earth it sings,
The rain it calls, the pests it stills.
A balance held in fragile might,
A gift of love, a world in flight.
